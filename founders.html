Stage 01

Most Opportunities Don't Fail Because the Business Is Bad

They fail because something gets in the way. A projection that doesn't tie to the story. A structure that isn't obvious. A key question that goes unaddressed because no one thought to ask it internally.

What Gets In The Way

None of these kill a deal outright. They just make it harder to say yes to. And in a world where investors see dozens of opportunities a week, 'harder to say yes to' means 'won't happen.'

  • A projection that doesn't tie to the story
  • A structure that isn't obvious to a reader
  • A key question that goes unaddressed internally
  • Materials that don't work together as one position
Where deals stall
What Investors Actually See
  • Dozens of opportunities reviewed each week
  • First impressions happen fast — and don't reset
  • Gaps in logic signal broader problems
  • Serious opportunities look serious from page one

Stage 03

Materials That Work Together

Sometimes that means a full deck from the ground up. Sometimes it means revising what exists. Either way, the goal is the same: materials that work together as one coherent position — not a collection of slides and spreadsheets.

The Core Materials
  • Pitch deck that communicates with clarity and restraint
  • Financials that can be explained and defended in plain terms
  • Supporting materials that provide depth where needed
  • One coherent position across every document
External Presence
  • Website and deal room that carries the same logic throughout
  • Teaser materials for early-stage conversations
  • Consistent professional form at every touchpoint
  • Confidence that nothing will pull an investor out of the deal
